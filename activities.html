Bowling

Rowans, Finsbury Park

A kitsch retro-style bowling alley with pool tables, arcades and sports screens, Rowans has stood the test of time. Even if you’re not a fan of bowling, there are plenty of other things you can do here including a karaoke booth and chilling by the bar. At weekends, the dance floor gets packed out while the DJs drop some classic tunes. Open late on Fridays/Saturdays, you can have decent night out without going out out.

Admission fees £1 except after 8pm Fridays and Saturdays where it's £3, bowling prices per person range between £4.50-£6

Crazy Golf

Plonk Golf, various London locations

Found across London, Plonk takes you on a 9-hole glow in the dark miniature crazy golf adventure with mechanical parts and active volcanos! The UV neon colours and smoke machines transport you into another world. Set up alongside vintage video arcade games such as pinball, ping pong and radio car racing, these indoor activities will keep you putting for hours.

Dalston £12, Brixton (Duke of Edinburgh Pub) £12, London Fields (BYOB policy) £8, Battersea £6

Board Games

Draughts, Haggerston

London’s first board game café with 600+ games, this place can keep you playing for hours. Whether you are looking to play solo or in groups, and whatever your game of choice, they will find the board for you together with a delicious board of charcuterie, mezze and cheeses. Snakes and ladders pros, scrabble word masters, battleship captains, and kerplunk marble maniacs, come here, challenge yourself and your friends to a healthy dose of competition.

Ping Pong

Bounce, Old Street/Farringdon

Based in two locations in East Central London, Bounce has exceeded expectations by providing a whole new meaning to ping pong. It is a perfect way to get your heart rate going to especially build an appetite for their incredibly mouth-watering wood burning oven cooked pizzas and delectable cocktails. Covering 17 tables the venues have been so creatively designed for this exciting experience that you can enjoy with friends and strangers.

Plants

Barbican Conservatory

Tired of cold and wet London, let the Conservatory takes you to the hot home of 2,000 tropical plants and breathe in that oxygen. Hidden in the heart of the Barbican centre, this oasis is the second largest conservatory in London where you can just immerse yourself in its beauty, observing a variety of plant species. Daily personal tours are also held by the resident gardeners where you can learn of its history and about the plants.

Axe Throwing

Whistle Punks, Vauxhall

It's nice to do something different once in a while, and competitive axe throwing seems to be it. The concept is simple: you throw axes. You get placed into one of two teams and then go on to compete against each other in a tournament hurling hatchets at a wooden target at the end of the range. Don't worry, you will be taught exactly how to throw that axe before you wound yourself or others. Backed with great music and the chance to socialise between throws, this is one of the most satisfying social day or night out you'll have.

£22 off-peak, £29 peak

Darts

Flight Club, Shoreditch

Re-defining everything you think you know about darts, Flight Club is a real fun-time bar. They have blown apart the classic pub game of darts using ground-breaking technology with animated scoring to transform it into a new game-play. Social darts has eliminated the dodgy maths and old-fashioned writing on chalkboards, making it a really fun and sociable experience. Add in a cool cocktail bar and sharing dishes, this is a great way to keep entertained when its pouring outside.

An oche costs about £30 per hour with up to 12 people playing at once, each oche holds up to 20 guests
